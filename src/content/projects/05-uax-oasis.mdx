The insight was simple: students buy coffee every day, but rarely feel any connection to the institution serving it. OASIS turns that transaction into a visible act of community.

## Design Language

Inspired by the classic Mediterranean coffee cart: warm materials, open form, immediately welcoming. Powder-coated steel frame with reclaimed wood accents. Large-format impact counter (cups served → euros generated → scholarships funded) integrated directly into the cart's face.

## The Social Mechanism

Every cup served at €1.50 channels €0.50 into a scholarship fund managed by Filantrópico. QR receipts let buyers see the cumulative impact in real time. That feedback loop is what makes habitual buying feel like habitual giving.

## Replicability

The cart is designed for flat-pack shipping and 2-hour assembly with standard tools. Event version collapses to fit a standard van. The design system (graphics, signage, impact counter) is templated so any campus or event can deploy it without custom work.

## Impact

After the cohort review, the professor confirmed our design was
**among the two finalists** under consideration. The kiosk was
designed so that **one third of every coffee's profit** goes
directly to the university's scholarship and education programs,
turning a daily transaction into recurring philanthropy without
changing the user's behavior.
