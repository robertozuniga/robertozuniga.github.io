Outdoor communities in Spain had plenty of Instagram accounts but no shared infrastructure — no unified way to organize meetups, coordinate logistics, or find people who matched your pace and ambition. PLAN Z App was built to fix that.

## Design Thinking Process

Five phases: observation (interviewing active community members), synthesis (affinity mapping and persona definition), ideation (competitive analysis + 80-sketch sprint), prototyping (three Figma iterations), and testing (usability sessions with real users from the target demographic).

## Key Features

- **Events hub**: create, discover, and RSVP to outdoor activities with route previews and gear recommendations
- **Community chat**: topic-based group threads with location sharing for active outings
- **Tools integration**: weather, elevation profiles, and emergency contacts all in one place

## System Design

Delivered as a full Figma design system: components, variants, interaction states, spacing tokens, and a handoff-ready spec document. The engineering team could start building from day one.

## Impact

PLAN Z is a real outdoor adventure community we built and still
operate (currently paused while I focus on Simplia). We organized
**outdoor plans with 100+ people total**, including **mountain
trips with groups of 30+** taking people into nature who wouldn't
otherwise have gone. We grew the brand from **0 to 5,400+
followers across Instagram, YouTube, and TikTok in 8 months** —
fully **organic, zero ad spend** — and produced one tracking-skills
video on reading natural signs to follow trails that crossed
**one million views**. The team: me leading product and brand,
plus an engineer partner and a tourism student.

I led the **complete design and prototype of the PLAN Z app**: all
product screens, user flows, wireframes, brand identity, and the
full Figma prototype — applying Design Thinking end-to-end from
discovery through prototype.
