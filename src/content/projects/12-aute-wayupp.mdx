## The brief

WayUpp's first version achieved something that didn't exist before:
a single national database of every Catholic youth community,
retreat, and volunteer opportunity in Spain, browsable by city.
Real users download it. Real parishes register through it. The
product works.

The redesign is about taking that working core and asking what it
could be when designed by someone who treats the user — a young
person uncertain about whether they belong inside something this
old — as the central design constraint, not the institution.

## My role

Product Designer at **Aute** since January 2025 (started as
internship, continuing as ongoing volunteer collaboration). I work
alongside another designer on the WayUpp redesign — leading user
research, heuristic evaluation, information architecture,
wireframes, prototyping, and testing.

## What we've shipped so far

The first major redesign milestone: a **completely rebuilt
onboarding flow** for parishes, movements, and retreats — the
three groups that need to register their realities into WayUpp
for young users to find them.

The previous onboarding was complex enough that many providers
abandoned mid-flow. Through iterative design sprints and
continuous testing, we **reduced the complexity of the onboarding
funnel by approximately 3.4×** (estimated, based on number of
steps and friction points removed).

## Impact

WayUpp serves **1,000+ active monthly users** as part of **Aute's
broader 80,000+ community** across the foundation's programs. The
redesigned onboarding flow is **3.4× simpler** than the original
(estimated, based on the reduction in steps and friction points),
making it possible for non-technical parish leaders, movement
coordinators, and retreat organizers to register their groups
themselves. The redesign is ongoing — new versions are released
continuously as we test and iterate.
