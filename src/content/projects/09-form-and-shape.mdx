Everything starts with a sketch. This collection is about understanding form before technology, trusting the hand before the mouse, and seeing before solving.

## Graphite Studies

Realistic rendering using graphite on smooth Bristol board. Studies of light and shadow across complex geometric and organic forms. The goal is not photorealism. It is understanding how values build volume.

## Acrylic Explorations

Acrylic on canvas, ranging from representational still-life studies to fully abstract color field experiments. Each piece is a study in composition, palette limitation, and gestural mark-making.

## Marker & Ink

Industrial design-style marker renderings using Copic markers, the standard language of product design sketching. These serve double duty: they train rapid visualization and document ideas that will become 3D models.

## Shape Studies

Pure form exploration on paper: positive/negative space, figure-ground relationships, and geometric transformations. This practice informs every product design decision that follows.
